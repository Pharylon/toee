The Dwarven Battlemage

 

The Battle mage is a character that has devoted his or her life to the art of Magic and Warfare.  They often weave spells in the midst of battle aiding friends and felling foes with their expert weapon strikes. While they have the best of both worlds in a sense, they are not as powerful as either a Wizard or a fighter would be of and equal level.  However the 1-2 combination punch of mind and brawn seems to be enough.

Requirements
To qualify as a Battlemage, you must meet the following requirements
Abilities:  Must have an intelligence of 19 or higher.
Skills:  Concentration: 5 ranks
Feats:  Expertise, Weapon Focus in any axe, combat casting, any one metamagic feat, Medium Armor Proficiency, Shield Proficiency, and proficiency in any axe.
Spells:  Must be able to cast arcane spells of 2nd level or higher.
Other:  Base Attack Bonus of 3+, must be a dwarf

 

Class Skills
Climb (Str), Concentration (Con), Craft (Int), Jump Str), Knowledge (any) (Int), Listen (Wis), Profession (Wis), Scry (Int, exclusive skill), Spellcraft (Int), and Tumble (Dex)

Skill Points at Each Level 2 + Int modifier.

 

Class Features
All of the following are class features of the Battlemage

Hit Die:  d6

Weapon and Armor Proficiency:  Battlemages gain no new weapon or armor proficiencies.

Ability Descriptions:

 

Bolster Spell: (su) By concentrating for a full round before casting any spell, the caster can raise the DC of said spell by 5.  Concentration check must be made with any usual penalties for outside interference for that round.

Spell Storing: (su) The Dwarven Battle Mage is able to prepare a spell in a weapon as if he/she had the spell storing ability on that weapon.  The Dwarven Battle Mage must be proficient with the weapon.  The Dwarven Battle Mage may store any one targeted spell of up to third level in the weapon.  (the spell must have a casting time of one action) Any time the weapon strikes a creature and that creature takes damage, the weapon can immediately cast the spell as a free action if the wielder so desires.  (This is a special exception to the general rule that casting a spell from an item takes at least as long as casting it normally.)

Dwarven Fortitude: (Ex)  When in the negatives, instead of rolling percentile chance to stabilize, the Dwarven Battle Mage makes a Fortitude saving Throw DC 20

Dwarven Toughness: (Ex) This is just like the feat in sword and Fist.  The Dwarven Battle Mage gains an extra 6 HP.

Improved Counterspell: (sp) This is just like the Feat in Forgotten Realms Handbook.
