The Exploits of Julin Sander, et al and the Third Rise of the Temple of Elemental Evil

(working title)

       In the days past the forces of evil have always been many and powerful, and few have been the years of Oerth that has not seen strife and conflict between those forces, and the forces of good and justice. So it was in those days that a cult of great evil did form in the lands south of The Olve Nation of Celene and the Great Lake Nyr Dyv in the land known to its people as Verbobonc, under the worship of a dark god called the Elder Elemental Eye, and their minions did blacken the land with filth and death, and the people did cringe and weep, and cry out for heroes.  As is the way of such things, heroes rose, from the common people they came and twice they threw down the dark worshipers and their minions and for many years the people remembered what it was to live in peace.  Families were raised, homes were built, and men prospered.  But men are often foolish, and time dulls the pain of old wrongs done, and has happens, people forgot.  The watch slackened on the Terrible Temple; slackened, faltered, and ultimately failed.  Evil was allowed to return.  Again strife rent the land asunder, and great Heroes were needed.  The tale of the Second Rise of The Temple of Elemental Evil is a great one, full of noble deeds, and great wonders, as well as terrible tragedies.  Such a tale cannot be told in full here, but suffice to know that the Evil was once again driven out, and again the land slept quite.  And again, men forgot.

       Thus once more, did evil rise, for evil cannot truly be driven out, only subdued, and the story that was told in the days of the Second Rise now simply continues, for the true stories do not end, only come to the present for those living then to join them.  And in the present in those days evil was waxing again, and the people cried out.  Thus did heroes rise in the land of Verbobonc, between the mountains, and the Lake.  Already they were great men, fresh from victory driving the evil from the Sunless Citadel.  Long from now will glasses be raised in toast to their names, common in the mind as household words, they will ring through the ages. 

     The Great Elf Numenarh.  Strong as a Bear and quicker and slyer than a Fox, he stood bright and tall.   Of such great skill with the bow he was that he could pluck the eye from a sparrow in mid-flight.  And so keen of sight he could track the same on a cloudy day. 

     Quadro, the Great Dwarven Battle-Mage.  His shoulders were wide as a Wagon Spit, and his arms strong as the roots of the old mountains and his Axe gleamed even in the darkness and hungered for the blood of wicked things, yet as keen as his axe was, even keener was his mind, and the mysteries of the unseen were laid bare before his most casual attention.  Never has a Dwarf before known so many great and secret things, and never shall they again, not though the Lortmils themselves crumble with age, and the seas dry up under the Sun. 

     Mewn Riverflow, also traveled with them.  A Rouge of legendary skill she was able to disappear like a wisp of smoke in a hurricane, and silent as the Quag on a moonless midnight.  Quick enough to slip the gold from the pocket of a king on his throne surrounded by a hundred guards, yet so beautiful that all she needed do was but ask and it would be hers.

     Tyro the Wild, a Druid of great power, joined the hero’s in Hommlet.  Raised by Wolves as a child, all the beasts of the field were his to command and he in his eyes showed back the untamed fury of the creatures he commanded.  As the subjects are to a Lord, so were all the forests animals to he.  In battle he fought like a Bear, ferocious and unyielding, strong and deadly.  Lastly, I, the great Julin Sander, Bard Extraordinaire, and teller of this tale, traveled as a Hero, aiding with a quick sword and a voice of Magic.  Assisting the heroes was a Cleric of St. Cuthbert known as Rask, who ministered to both souls and wounds, and to who’s holy presence no Undead could stand, and a young child named Meepo, who the Heroes had rescued from Kobolds, and of whom we were caring for

     Such were the heroes that battled in the Third Rise of the Temple of Elemental Evil.  The story is a great one, full of Magic and Mystery, Betrayal and Honor; it is a story of Heroes.  Let it begin.

 

       The story begins as so many other great tales begin, with a simple task, to be performed over the span of a few days.  The Companions and I arrived in the small town known as Hommlet, after being hired by the Druid Tyro to look into the strange doings of his current master, who was now missing.  In those days the power of the Temple was only beginning to grow, and men still spoke lazily in afternoons and took no mind of the foul shrine so close to them, for they felt safe, thinking the evil in it long dead.  The only thought ever given the Temple was that Gnolls had taken up residence in the Temple Proper, and men grumbled that they should someday soon be removed least they become a nuisance.  Had they been less complacent they would have seen such an event for what it was, for that was the beginning of the re-population of the Temple with Foul Things. 

     However, the Companions felt things were amiss, and following leads, traveled to the Moathouse of the Temple, the least of it’s outbuildings, to search for the old missing druid.  A single days ride they took from the civilization of Hommlet, to this Moathouse, a building to which its neighbors gave no mind, and thought of as a relic of the past. 

     During this time the Elf Numenarh took a short leave of the Companions, for during this peaceful time even his great hero’s heart was complacent and he was confident of his friends’ strength, and his love waited for him in the grove of his home to wed him.   Thus the Companions went without him and did not know the danger into which they rode.  They approached the old Moathouse, ruined and broken.  The Moat, once swiftly flowing, was now stagnant and foul things lived there.  The woodwork had long since collapsed under the weight of age, only the stone walls stood as a hollow reminder of the past.   The ancient place looked like a tomb, and smelled like a charnel house.  A place for only the long dead, surely.

     However, all was not as it appeared.  After long riding it came to pass that Numenarh came upon an old tracker traveling the ways near Hommlet and the tracker beseeched the Elf to stop for he saw he had the look of a hero.

       “Halt please, noble Elf.  Surely,” said the ancient tracker, “you must be a great warrior, for none here carry such fine weaponry and such a noble mantle.” 

       “Yea,” replied Numenarh, “a hero I am, old father.  What service I may offer you is yours, though I bid you make haste, for my love waits for me and I would not tarry long.” he replied glancing anxiously northward.

     The old man grew anxious and spoke quickly “Surely great is indeed my need, for I passed a group of heroes riding West this very day, though they did not see me I think.  If they be friends of yours then I would bid you take a message to them as swift as you may, for they are riding into great danger.  Not two days before I saw something which turned my blood to ice:  A Dragon, it was, terrible in size and fury, and perilously close to where thy companions traveled.  Normally, for such heroes I would not fear, but they do not know the peril into which they go”.

       And a shadow fell over Numenarh’s heart, and he knew his friends were walking into great danger, and so forgetting his love, he turned and spurred his horse rode like the wind.

     But the Companions knew none of this and approached the Moathouse at ease, for many had told them it had been abandoned for long years and the townsfolk themselves often visited in the hopes of uncovering hidden wealth none had found for in long years since the evil was driven out.  Truly the Moathouse looked and seemed dead, and for good reason.  The Companions entered unafraid.

       As they entered into the Courtyard, littered with fallen stone and covered with the dust of ages, did the Dragon come.  It came from the sky like the roar of a Waterfall, and with all the inevitability of sunset and it perched Itself atop the crumbling tower of the Moathouse.  Mighty it was, a great Old Wyrm.   Long of Tooth and hot with Fury.  Its scales were the blue of a clear pool and lightning seemed to crackle around it.  Its wings were like great Sails, and it claws like curv’ed lances.  As it sat atop the tower, the building listed and shuddered under its mighty girth, but the creature paid it no mind, and it addressed the Companions:

       “Begone!”  Its voice boomed like Thunder and the ground shoot with its power.  “This Land do I claim as my own and if you value your lives Heroes I would not tarry here long.  My temper is short and my wrath quick and you would not live to see the end of it, I think”. 

       But though the Dragon was old and Mighty, the Companions did not fear it, for such is the business of Heroes.  I stepped forward and spoke, “Foolish creature,” I said “we do not parley with the cruel likes of you.  Your choices are thus:  Begone from this place and darken this land no more, or die.  No other terms shall be acceptable”. 

       “Then you shall all Burn,” said it.

       Thus was battle met.  The Dragon plunged down from the tower, driving its terrible weight forward, it swept towards the companions, who met it with steel bared.  The battle that resulted was terrible, and the sound and Magic released could be heard and seen for miles around.  The Dragon swept past again and again, lashing out with tooth, claw and tail with each pass.  The Axe of Quadro was red with blood, and death flew from his fingertips.  Mewn’s arrows streaked forth in a hail, and even Rask called upon a holy Apparition to hary the beast.  And though the Heroes wounded it mightily, the fury of the dragon wore down on them as well, and none escaped without mark.  In time the cunning old Wyrm drew up above us and beat his mighty wings, so that the dust and sand and rocks rose into the air vision was obscured and nothing could be seen.  Then were things grim for the Companions, for the Dragon struck out with Lightning and Magic and they were hard set upon and their courage began to fail in the Darkness of the Dragon’s cloud.  It was then, in this dark time, that Numenarh came, riding like a Thunderbolt, bow singing a song of death.  His arrows found their mark again and again and the Wyrm drew back in fear at the fury of his coming.  But bested it was not, for it drew back it’s breath and let loose a mighty Lightning bolt from it’s mouth and it struck down Quadro and wounded Numenarh grievously, so that all thought both were slain and in their hearts a shadow grew and though they continued to stand against it as Heroes do they quailed thinking surely the end had come for them all.  But burned and broken Numenarh rose unsteadily, for blood was on his face and his armor and his steed lay dead, but rose he did and raised his bow, unruined against all odds, and leveled his last arrow against the great beast, who stood in wonder at his courage.

       “Foul Beast”, he spat “return to the dark realm that spawned you, for I will suffer you no more.  Your doom is come.” 

       And his arrow streaked forward and struck the Dragon in its neck, and the creature heaved forward for the last time and fell dead.  Thus did Numenarh, at the last moment possible, save his Companions from certain doom and remove a great evil from the world.
