To: Jenna my Fair Maiden

      C/O Jelanah

       Jeleket’s Roost

       Verbobnc City, Viscounty and Town of Verbobnc

 

 

 

 

My Dearest Jenna,

 

Seeing you again for just the second time makes me feel like we’ve always known each other, for your eternal beauty refreshes my soul as that of cold stream water to parched lips.  I sincerely do understand that in your current profession, that you have no time for love.  I also understand that the gentlemen and the scalliwags that you administer to care not one copper piece for your emotional affections or to return them.

I make no bones about my deep feelings for you.  The first time we were together, I felt a certain closeness with you that was never there for any of the other women I had past bed.  Although you happen to be human makes not a bone.  You have a serene eminence about you that pervades your movement and verbiage.  I suppose I alone have noticed it.  I am the lucky one.  You my dear, though unbeknownst to you at the time, are just as, if not more lucky.  

 

My last encounter with you was frustrating.  Wholly in part to the fact that you see me as an obsessed adventurer smitten with your wares.  Untrue.  I care nothing of your wares…well what I mean to say is, I like you for you, and not what you have physically given me.  Maybe on the outside you see me as just another scruffy rogue wanderer.  Let me tell you a little about the Quadro Gemini that you may not know.

 

I was born Targin Gemana 60 years ago in the foothills of the Yatil Mountains, 15 miles south of a town called Krestible.  My Mother Callisene and my father Raff raised 18 children, one male and the rest female.  My parents were very attentive and doting of their only son, and instilled early on the virtue of good over evil.   My father, a barbarian from the local Kraka Clan, encouraged me to become a fierce Warrior.  He would take me to the surface to hunt and practice fighting skills as early as eight years of age.  Even at such an early time period my father said that I was a quick learner, a prodigious student if you will.  I one time felled a brown bear with my bare hands at the age of 23.  I fought hard, but back underground, I studied even harder.  My mother was always secretly supportive of my book knowledge and the desire to learn.  It was around this time that she would tell me stories of Human and Elven wizards that wielded the mysterious mental powers of Magic.  I yearned to discover all I could learn about this ‘magic’.  On solo hunting excursions on the surface I would trek to Krestible to learn more.  Wondrous Lore was a magic shoppe on the outskirts of town run by a lady named Circe Orion.  Forced to a locale on the outskirts of town due to bigger and better shoppes elsewhere, Circe still had plenty of tomes for an eager mind.  She imparted me a few books on apprenticeship and bid me farewell.  Hurrying back to the caves in the hills I had all but made up my mind.  Magic was much more to me than just a passing fancy.  It was a yearning, a destiny of sorts.  I would pack my things and leave home that night.  My fighting skills though not honed to perfection would help me in the quest to find a skilled wizard that would take me on as an apprentice.  Though, the decision had been made for me by the time I had arrived home.  My father had ransacked my quarters only to find the books on Magic I had been reading.  Utterly furious with me, he banished me from his home for five years.

 

To this day it’s been thirty-seven years since I’ve been back.

 

I went back to see Circe.  She let me stay in the back of the shoppe for free for awhile.  Almost two years.  She was too busy to take me on as a study, but I sure did read a lot.  She talked about a wizard that was taking apprentices.

His moniker was Bartok the Noble.  He was actually a priest who knew a fine bit about the arcane arts as well.  She said that I could find him south in the land of Veluna though she did not know exactly where.  It was my goal to find him.  Through a 12-year ordeal of trial and turmoil of which I care not to discuss at present, I finally found Bartok in a city called Mitrik.  Bartok was not taking apprentices anymore by this time, but found something interesting enough in me to invite me to stay.  Bartok was a gray-haired Human, somewhere in the neighborhood of 50 or 60 years old.  He was sharp although frail looking. Perhaps it was my stature that caused him to consider it, to take me on as a bodyguard of sorts, hell I don’t know.

All I know is from that moment on….things were not that great.  Bartok made me sign an agreement.  A contract to fifteen years of indentured servitude, and in return, he would teach me the arts.  Well, I thought, fifteen years isn’t so bad for a Dwarf, but will he live that long.  As it turns out…almost.  First thing Bartok did, was change my name.  If I was going to be a wizards apprentice, I had to fully change my birth name and alter my surname to something more ‘regally upbeat’ is how he put it.  He gave me the name Quadro.  It was an odd sounding name, but he said that it was derived from the four elements that I as a mage, would eventually draw power from.  Gemini was my new last name, a small hop skip- and a jump from Gemana, but it upset me that I was changing my family’s heritage to suit some cantankerous old coot, but I told myself, it’s all or nothing.  If I am gonna learn, I might as well put my head down and go with the flow.  So Quadro Gemini I was, Housekeeper.  At least that’s what I believed my title to be for the better part of the first five years.  Loyalty is what he said I should be demonstrating, and then we would learn magic.  I was not allowed to leave the house either.  One of his rules.  He claimed that true studies need not be concerned with the outside world, only the boundaries of the mind.  Oft-times Bartok would be gone for 12 sometimes 14 hours of a day, only to return weary and retire to his study for the eve.  I did learn though.  I pored through old tomes and historical books in his library when he wasn’t home and when I wasn’t cleaning up after his nasty ass.  I learned a lot about the layout and the history of the Flanaess.  I longed to journey out and explore.  Over the course of the next few years, I got Bartok the Messy to teach me all sorts of cantrips.  It was then that he permitted me to venture out into the front or backyards to practice.  Cantrips gone awry, he used to say, were recipes for a new house.  I one time broke his mailbox with a flubbed prestidigitation spell.  Over the next few years I had begun to master the cantrips he had given me, and I yearned for bigger and better spells.  Quadro, he would say, you must fully feel and be able to control the energy that flows through you before we proceed.  He said it destroyed many a mage before me.  Gosh, I was so frustrated at how slow this was going. 

 

One day in the year…oh it must have been about 581 cy, I was snooping through his bed quarters and I stumbled upon a different sort of book.  A diary.  I nervously began to read, all the while a knot had twisted up the bottom of my stomach that told me repeatedly I shouldn’t be in here.  The diary laid out Bartok’s life.  Well for the hours he was gone in the day, Bartok was out attending something called the College of Bishops.  It also revealed a password that I didn’t understand, the word was Azgharzak.  I stood up and said it aloud.  With an eerie hiss, and the ringing of a bell, Bartok’s bookcase vanished and left a descending stairway in its wake.  Fearing Bartok would be home soon, I closed and replaced the diary and repeated the word again to make the bookcase reappear.  Nothing.  I tried to find a switch of some sort to close it, but no luck.  I went back to the cellar hoping that it would close on it’s own, or maybe that Bartok would think that he left it open.  Well night came and went, and Bartok didn’t say anything about it.  Hmmm.  I crept back into his room to find the bookcase reappeared and everything as I had found it the previous day.  I dug out the diary again to remember the word and repeated it once more to the same effect.  Down the stairs was a chamber of sorts whereupon the floor was a giant star inside a circle.  Black candles filled the chamber, some aglow, some snuffed out.  The walls were also covered in books, but not on any shelf.  They appeared to be held in place by entwining kudzu.  How odd.  I started to realize that there was more to Bartok than I had imagined.  Over the last few seasons, he had grown more irritable and gruff with me if not downright hostile at times.  This place which reeked of evil and despair and a slight scent of Brimstone was obviously his continual undoing.  I read some of the tomes down there and learned that Azgharzak was the name of an ancient Half-dragon Wizard turned demon.  Apparently this was some sort of shrine to him.  Over the next year and a half, I fully mastered the cantrips I was shown and Bartok was begrudgingly teaching me first level spells.  I finally had my own Spell book now, instead of writing everything down on loose pieces of parchment.  As usual I went outside to practice them and thankfully I did.  Although Bartok the Grumpy didn’t get too upset when I burned down his fence with a not-quite-right shocking grasp spell.

My routine became ..well routine.  Everyday I had snuck into Bartok’s quarters  after he left and would read his entry into his diary.  I was horrified at how dismally he viewed the world and people in general.  Almost viscious in his thoughts.  My anger and bubbling contempt for Bartok grew, but I said nothing for weeks and months.  Until one day, he had eluded to a ritual that would inherently bring Azgharzak forth from the outer planes and help him raze Mitrik and soon control the whole continent.  Almost immediately, my bubbling contempt had transformed into seething anger and hatred towards the man I had long considered my Mentor.  I knew at once that Bartok must die.  Tonight.  By my hand.  So soon as he slept that eve.  I crept into his room with my dagger, and slit his throat ear to ear.  Blood flowed over the edge of the bed pooling on the floor.  The blood droplets made grotesque shadows on the wall of the candlelit room.  When it was over, I felt nothing.  Not remorse, not relief, just nothing.  I let the dagger slip from my hand and I followed it with my eyes as it tumbled in slow motion to the floor where it stuck point first into the wood.  Blood oozing down the blade to form a separate puddle around the knife.  Murder.  The word murder rang over and over in my head.  The realization hit me that I had just took another's life in cold blood.  Even though it was highly justified, it was still a crime punishable by death.  That night I packed up my things and left town.  Over the next few years I wandered from village to village along the banks of the Velverdyva River.

I had realized since no one ever really knew who I was for the 14 years I had stayed in Mitrik, I almost needn’t worry about getting caught.  I slowly began to feel a sense of Justice, especially as I looked into the faces of the elderly shopkeepers in these villages.  I knew they wouldn’t be here today if it weren’t for me.  One day, not long ago, I entered a village called Oakhurst.  There was a lady inside offering a reward for the return of some missing persons.  I needed some adventure in me and I went to sign up for the task.  It was there and then when I met most of the group I travel with now.  Numenarh Daerkwood, a sly, softspoken yet powerful ranger whom I am teaching a bit about the arts.  Mewn Riverflow, an Elven rogue by design, I trust her no less than any other party member.  She’s sweet although a bit clumsy for a woman in her profession.  A mighty cleric of St. Cuthbert named Rask the Destroyer.  This guy can hang with the undead like the best of them.  One look at his holy symbol and bam….no skeletons.  Julin Sandar, performer extrordinaire.  He is a flamboyant fellow who likes to haggle with shopkeepers and sing an uplifting tune to help us in battle.  If it weren’t for his penchant to start arguments, he’d be my favorite.  The newcomers to our party are Grawl, a good gnoll and Tyro, a druid.  I have yet to learn much from them, but I will as the days go by.

 

So you see, my sweet Jenna, I have a story, a background, a life.  I trust you wholly, and I want you to trust me with your heart.  I think about you when the suns are up and in the dead of night.  I wish the best for you and I will write again soon and let you know how we are doing.

 

 

 

Lovingly Always,

Quadro Gemini

My Dearest Jenna,

 

 

 

Hello there buttercup, it has been awhile since I have written you, close to four months.  I am truly sorry for the delay, what with adventuring and stuff.  Since I have last talked to you, we as a group have accomplished many things.  Now this what I am about to tell you is very secret/hush hush, so I would appreciate it if you didn’t go running your lip about it, you know how you humans are, not to mention you women.  Anyway, we have invaded the temple of the Elemental Evil cultists, and smashed some skulls.  I have since been alected leader of the party and am doing a bang-up job.  Well, with the exception that some people died.  Some more than once.  Mewn had died, three times and on a completely unrelated note, she wont let me touch her breasts…go figure…anyway.  Our beloved Meepo, Goblin Slayer has passed on once, as has our friend Tyro’s first two wolves…I have a feeling there are going to be many more…shheeeesh.  On top of all that, our cleric got changed into a Dwarf for some time, he was looking kinda right if you know what I mean, just slap a dress on him and……..well getting back to me story.  I have been shot at, hacked up by some rogues, singed by fire and chilled by the breath of an ogre mage, and still I think of you. I have learned a few new skills that inhibit me from writing as much as I used to though.   I currently make magical items for our group as well as magic weapons.  Not your run-of-the mill adventurer am I now?  I know you tried to brush me off during out last encounter, but I was strong and I knew you needed time to get to know me.    When I gave that letter to Sandy at the Inn before we left Verbobnc that day, I knew that something was amiss between us, but I couldn’t put my finger on it.   And well with you having to deal with the death of Jelanah, for that I am sorry.  If there was anything that I could do, I would, but you know how humans are…dying so quick and she was really old too.  I mean really old, wasn’t she.  If she wasn’t, well she looked it.  Anyway, what I am trying to say…..hold on a sec, meepo is poking me with a stick…..Be right back…………………………………………………

……………………………..ok, I am back.  I had to give the little guy a noogie, but where was I?  Oh yeah, I just don’t think it’s going to work out between us….there, I said it.  Don’t cry, I know you are tore up inside, but there will be other men.  I’m not doing it for anyone else, I don’t have a turtle dove in the wings as so to speak, it’s just that, If I can’t see you, it’s wrong of me to expect you to stay faithful, so I am cutting your string fair Jenna.  I have enjoyed the time we spent together and I don’t regret the money I had to spend to do it either.   I will find someone else in due time, as well do not worry.  I will make something for you in the near future and send it your way for you to always think of me.  Take care of You now dear.  May Moradin always smile on your enemies and smash them.

 

 

 

Yours truly,

Quadro “sweetie rump” Gemini
