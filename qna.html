Questions and Answers

 

Q:  What is this site?

A:  Ah, that's an easy one.  This site is designed to be a resource for Planescape players in converting the Planescape campaign setting to Third Edition.  

 

Q:  How often is it updated?

A:  One per week.  Usually.

 

Q:  What's a "Kriegstanz?"

A:  The Kriegstanz is the undeclared war for the hearts and minds of Sigil and the Outer Planes.  It's mostly a political one, fought with words and finances, but it sometimes spills over into out-and-out bloodshed.

 

Q:  Is this stuff balanced?

A:  I think so.  But to be honest, I haven't playtested most of it.  I think it's a bit more balanced than a lot of the stuff you'll find on the Internet (or even some of the 3rd party companies), but not as much so as, say, actual D&D stuff published by WotC.  I'm looking for feedback, though, so if you think something is too powerful, let me know.

 

Q:  Does joining a faction give me any special benefits?

A:  Not immediately, except the obvious benefits of having a lot of new friends, belonging to a large organization, and other non-mechanic stuff.  However, joining a faction does give you access to faction feats and prestige classes. 

 

Q:  In Second Edition, you got faction benefits for free

A:  Well, this isn't second edition, now is it?  In Second Edition, you got all kinds of stuff for free in the form of kits, factions, and whatnot.  In Third, you have to spend something to get those abilities, and the currency is feats and levels.  That means you have to take faction feats or prestige classes. However, if you really want, you may instate an optional rule that allows characters to take one faction feat for free when they join a faction.  It should be noted that I don't endorse this rule, however.  It forsakes game balance to be more faithful to second edition Planescape.

 

Q:  Feat X is too powerful!

A:  I don't believe it is.  Some feats are slightly better than their counterparts in the Core Rules, but like the regional feats of Forgotten Realms these feats can be slightly more attractive due being limited to only a small number of characters.  However, if you really think a feat is too powerful, email me at zach@zachshuford.com and I'd be more than happy to discuss it. I'm looking for criticism after all, and my primary concern is game balance.

 

Q:  What happened to faction disadvantages?

A:  Those are gone, for the most part.  Since the factions get nothing for free under my system, they don't get any special hindrances either.  And to be quite honest, the disadvantages in second edition Planescape were often very arbitrary or nonsensical and were just there in an attempt to balance the factions.  Some feats, however, reintroduce certain hindrances, like the Transcendent Order's feat Action Trance.

 

Q:  Why doesn't X ability work the same as it did in second edition?

A:  Probably because my first goal is game balance and my secondary goal is being faithful to second edition.

 

Q:  Why do some factions have lots of stuff on them and others don't?

A:  Originally, I was doing whole factions at a time, but I only got as far as the Dustmen, Harmonium, and the Godsmen before I got burnt out.  Now I'm just doing one update of some type per week.

 

Q:  Is there some way to find the new stuff quick?

A:  Sure there is, just click on the "New" link on the front page, it'll take you right to the new stuff.

 

Q:  What happened to all the post-Faction War stuff that used to be here.

A:  Um, to be honest I don't think anyone was really interested in it, so I ditched it.  If you are interested, however, let me know and I'll email it to you.  ;)

 

Q:  Do you accept submissions?

A:  Sure, just email me at zach@zachshuford.com and we'll work something out.  I'd especially like some artwork, but I'm really open to articles, prestige classes, or even just one new feat.
