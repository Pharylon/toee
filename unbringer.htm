The Unbringer

 

                Unbringers are very rare.  To become an unbringer a character must have suffered one or more personal tragedies as a direct result of undead (See prerequisites) activity.  The death of family or close friends is what usually causes an Unbringer to ultimately come to being.

            The overwhelming guilt and hollow feeling of loss transforms into an aura that surrounds the Unbringer at all times.  This aura is often nicknamed the Death Wish by Unbringers and the few, if any, friends they have.  This aura acts as a “well of negativity” that seems to radiate vast negative energy.  In reality it sheds no such energies but instead is the supernatural manifestation of the need to martyr and ultimately die.  Unbringers seek to destroy all the undead they possibly can before their lives are claimed by the undead their aura attracts or by the populace of some town or city.  The Unbringer constantly attracts undead with his Despair Aura and as such the populace of any place is subject to be attacked by all manner of undead.  Often people gather to either drive out or kill the Unbringer to end the Despair Aura and its deadly effect on the area the Unbringer resides.  

            An Unbringer that hopes to live even a short while seeks abandoned places to dwell.  The Unbringer as a rule lives a lonesome life filled with bouts against ever increasing amounts of undead.  Even stalwart friends and family usually can’t handle the constant dangers combined with the reclusive nature that must be any Unbringer life.  Very little is written about or known of Unbringers.  A knowledge history, Bardic knowledge or Lore check (DC28) can allow a character to know something of an Unbringer.  Knowledge is still from fables or obscure writing that was never confirmed as non-fiction.

Requirements
To qualify as a Unbringer, you must meet the following requirements
Skills:  Knowledge (Undead) 8 ranks, Craft (weaponsmithing) 8 ranks
Feats:  Iron Will, Extra Turning
Other:  Base Will save of 5 or greater, Must have suffered a deep personal tragedy dealing with undead.

 

Class Skills
Concentration (Con), Craft (Int), Diplomacy (Cha), Heal (Wis), Knowledge (Arcana) (Int), Knowledge (Religion), Knowledge (Undead) (Int), Profession (Wis), Scry (Int, exclusive skill), Spellcraft (Int), and Wilderness Lore (Wis)

Skill Points at Each Level 2 + Int modifier.

 

Class Features
All of the following are class features of the Unbringer

Hit Die:  d8

Weapon and Armor Proficiency:  Unbringers gain no new weapon or armor proficiencies.

Critical Undead:  Summoning positive energy, an Unbringer may deal a critical hits to the undead.  Whevever he rolls a result that would be a critical hit against the non-undead, the Unbringer may spend a turning attempt for the day to treat is as a critical threat, as he would against a normal creature.
  

Turn Stacking: The Unbringer continues to advance in the ability to turn undead as a normal cleric.  All levels of Unbringer stack with those of the Cleric for the purposes of turning undead.

Positive Aura: The Unbringer and all allies within 30' of him gain a bonus to saves versus energy draining affects equal to his wisdom modifier.

Empowered Cure:  The Unbringer's Cure spells are always considered Empowered whenever used against Undead.
  
Empowered Turning: At 10th level, the Unbringer always turns as if he were using the Empowered Turning feat from Defenders of the Faith.
